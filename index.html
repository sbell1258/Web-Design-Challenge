The Weather Report

Latitude vs Temperature.

The purpose is to showcase the relationship between Temperature (F) vs. Latitude. This images shows as expected that the temperatures are lot more warmer as the city proximity is closer to the equator.

Latitude vs Humidity.

The purpose is to showcase the relationship between Humidity (%) vs. Latitude. Humidity, because warm air can hold more moisture than cool air, locations with warm climates, such as those near the equator, generally have higher humidity than locations with cooler climates, such as those near the poles.

Latitude vs Cloudiness.

The purpose is to showcase the relationship between Cloudiness (%) vs. Latitude. There are more clouds at the equator! As warm, moist air converges at lower altitudes near the equator, it rises and cools and therefore can hold less moisture. This causes water vapor to condense into cloud particles and produces a dependable band of thunderstorms.

Latitude vs Windspeed.

The purpose is to showcase the relationship between Wind Speed (mph) vs. Latitude. Most (approximately 85-90%) of the cities presented here have wind speeds of 4 mph or less.
